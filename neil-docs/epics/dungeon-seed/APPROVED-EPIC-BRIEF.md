> **Status**: Approved

> **Epic**: Dungeon Seed
> **Type**: RPG / Idle / Progression
> **Platform Target**: PC first, mobile follow-up, console stretch goal
> **Engine Recommendation**: Godot 4.x

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Dungeon Seed is an RPG-leaning idle progression experience where the player is a mystical gardener of ruin and reward. Players plant magical seeds that sprout into living, procedurally generated dungeons. Those dungeons grow over time, absorb ambient magic, and eventually become ready for a team of adventurers to enter, explore, and harvest loot.

The game blends:
- high-level strategy and growth management,
- procedural dungeon creation,
- adventurer expedition planning,
- loot-driven progression,
- and idle time-based pacing.

This epic brief outlines the vision, player goals, core loops, progression systems, world design, art and audio direction, monetization guardrails, accessibility goals, and risk profile for Dungeon Seed.

---

## 2. Vision & Player Promise

### Core Fantasy

The player fantasy is:
- **The Architect of Adventure**: I shape a forest of living dungeons with every seed I plant.
- **The Strategic Caretaker**: I manage growth windows, choose mutations, and time expeditions.
- **The Reward Hunter**: I send heroes into my creations to return with rare loot and powerful resources.

### Emotional Promise

Players should feel:
- A calm satisfaction while waiting for dungeons to mature.
- A thrill when watching seed traits unlock new dungeon possibilities.
- A sense of agency when choosing which adventurers and gear to send.
- A loop of progression and compounding power as both seeds and adventurers evolve.

### Unique Selling Proposition

Dungeon Seed is a unique hybrid that makes dungeon creation itself the core progression mechanic. Instead of only exploring pre-made halls, players cultivate the source of the dungeon and influence its form through seed choice, growth conditions, and mutation paths.

## 3. Core Loops

### Micro Loop (30 seconds)

1. Inspect a seed or dungeon progress tile.
2. Apply a growth boost, mutation, or fertilizing resource.
3. Start an expedition or speed up a dungeon stage.
4. Watch adventurers clear a room, loot rewards appear, and resources stack.
5. Return to base and allocate loot toward seed upgrades or adventurer gear.

### Meso Loop (5 minutes)

1. Plant new seeds and assign growth strategies.
2. Review dungeon status, harvest idle rewards, and unlock new room types.
3. Recruit and equip adventurers for imminent runs.
4. Send teams into one or more dungeons.
5. Collect loot, spend resources, and unlock a deeper seed tier.

### Macro Loop (30+ minutes)

1. Advance through seed tiers and unlock new dungeon biomes.
2. Expand the seed garden and build a roster of specialist adventurers.
3. Discover and refine powerful dungeon mutation recipes.
4. Climb a progression track toward an upgraded home hub, stronger expeditions, and seasonal objectives.
5. Repeat with increasingly exotic seeds and richer loot goals.

### Flow Diagram

```
Plant Seed → Grow Dungeon → Monitor Progress → Dispatch Adventurers → Clear Rooms → Harvest Loot → Upgrade Seeds/Adventurers → Unlock New Seeds
```

## 5. Progression Systems

### Seed Progression

Seeds are the primary progression pillars. Each seed has:
- **Rarity tier**: Common, Uncommon, Rare, Epic, Legendary.
- **Elemental affinity**: Terra, Flame, Frost, Arcane, Shadow.
- **Growth profile**: Speed, density, mutation potential.
- **Dungeon traits**: room variety, hazard frequency, loot bias.

As seeds mature:
- their dungeons expand in size,
- new room types unlock,
- difficulty increases,
- loot quality and quantity improve.

### Dungeon Mutation

Seeds can be modified with mutation reagents. Example mutation paths:
- **Blooming Spires**: more treasure rooms, fewer traps.
- **Ironheart Ruins**: denser combat, higher armor enemy rewards.
- **Etheric Labyrinth**: more arcane hazards, rare resource drops.

Mutation is a long-term optimization layer. Players choose trade-offs between speed, safety, and reward type.

### Adventurer Progression

Adventurers have:
- **Classes**: Warrior, Ranger, Mage, Rogue, Alchemist, Sentinel.
- **Stats**: Health, Attack, Defense, Speed, Utility.
- **Experience**: earned from successful runs.
- **Level tiers**: Novice, Skilled, Veteran, Elite.

Progression unlocks:
- new abilities,
- higher equipment slots,
- stronger passive bonuses,
- advanced expedition roles (scout, support, escort).

### Loot & Economy

Loot fuels both seed and adventurer progression:
- **Gold**: currency for base upgrades and adventurer recruitment.
- **Essence**: used for seed growth boosts and mutations.
- **Fragments**: craft gear and special artifacts.
- **Artifacts**: rare items that grant permanent bonuses.

Economy design targets:
- a balanced faucet of rewards from idle and active play,
- meaningful sinks for progression and customization,
- no pay-to-win progression gates.

### Prestige & Mastery

Late-game systems include:
- **Garden Mastery**: earn bonus growth speed and loot bonuses from completing seed collections.
- **Endless Dungeon Tiers**: repeatable runs with scaleable rewards.
- **Seasonal Seed Trials**: temporary seeds with unique challenge rules.

## 6. World & Setting

### Core World Concept

The game world is a mystic garden of ruins and wild magic. Ancient seeds sprout not flora, but living dungeons that pulse with elemental power. The player is a Seedwarden, tending both the garden and the adventurer corps that harvests it.

### Key Locations

- **Seed Grove**: the player hub, where seeds are planted, nurtured, and analyzed.
- **Adventurer Hall**: a recruiting and training hall for heroes.
- **Mutarium Atelier**: where reagents are combined and seed mutations are crafted.
- **Vault & Market**: where loot is stored, sold, and converted.
- **Dungeon Interface**: a dynamic map of active dungeons and their growth state.

### Biome Progression

Each seed tier unlocks a new visual dungeon theme:
- **Verdant Grotto**: moss-clad caverns and crystal pools.
- **Ember Hollow**: magma veins and furnace halls.
- **Frostspire Keep**: icy vaults and frostbound corridors.
- **Aetherium Ruins**: floating chambers and arcane machinery.
- **Nightshade Vault**: shadow-wreathed sanctums and spectral traps.

### Narrative Hook

A forgotten garden has reawakened. The seeds left behind by an ancient civilization grow dungeons that hold both danger and the keys to restoring the world’s lost balance. The player must rebuild the garden, guide adventurers, and uncover why the dungeons have returned.

## 7. Art Direction

### Visual Style

- **Aesthetic**: stylized low-poly / painterly RPG with lush, hand-crafted textures.
- **Tone**: whimsical yet mysterious; rich colors with soft lighting and magical glows.
- **Character design**: charming campy adventurers with exaggerated silhouettes and expressive idle animations.
- **Dungeon design**: each biome has strong visual identity through color palette, architecture, and VFX.

### Palette & Mood

- **Seed Grove**: emerald green, warm gold, soft blues.
- **Verdant Grotto**: moss green, jade, amber glow.
- **Ember Hollow**: ember orange, charcoal, molten gold.
- **Frostspire Keep**: ice blue, silver, pale violet.
- **Aetherium Ruins**: cyan, violet, luminous white.
- **Nightshade Vault**: indigo, deep purple, spectral teal.

### UI/UX Visuals

- **Interface**: clean, layered panels with translucent glass and botanical flourishes.
- **Iconography**: readable symbols for seeds, resources, adventurers, and mutagens.
- **Feedback**: motion-rich UI transitions, glowing progress bars, subtle particle accents.

### Animation Priorities

- Seed planting and growth animations should feel alive and magical.
- Dungeon expansion reveals should evoke discovery and anticipation.
- Adventurer run animations should communicate class identity and energy.
- Loot collection and crafting animations should reward player actions with satisfying effects.

## 8. Audio Direction

### Music

- **Style**: ambient orchestral-electronic hybrid.
- **Mood**: calm and mysterious during growth, tense and rhythmic during expedition runs.
- **Adaptive layers**: separate tracks for idle hub, dungeon exploration, combat, and victory.

### Sound Effects

- **Seed planting**: soft earth chimes, sparkles, and root-sighs.
- **Dungeon growth**: creaking stone, blossoming energies, elemental whispers.
- **Adventurer actions**: distinct weapon swishes, spell casts, and class-specific impacts.
- **Loot/harvest**: satisfying chimes, resource collect plinks, and inventory confirms.

### Audio Language

- Use gentle, layered ambience in the Seed Grove.
- Increase percussion and tension when adventurers enter active rooms.
- Reinforce progression with musical cues when seeds mutate or reach full maturity.
- Keep SFX clear and non-fatiguing for longer idle sessions.

## 9. Monetization Principles

### Business Model

- Primary focus on ethically designed free-to-play with optional cosmetic and convenience purchases.
- Progression-affecting improvements must be earnable through gameplay.
- No pay-to-win.

### Purchasable Content

- **Cosmetic bundles**: hero outfits, seed packet skins, dungeon theme overlays.
- **Convenience**: inventory expansion, non-essential time accelerators, premium interface themes.
- **Battle pass / seasonal pass**: cosmetic rewards, unique seeds skins, and extra customization options.
- **Expansion packs**: future biomes, adventurer classes, and story chapters.

### Non-Purchasable Systems

- Seed rarity progression,
- adventurer leveling,
- core dungeon generation,
- loot quality,
- seed mutation access.

### Ethical Guardrails

- No locked content behind timed paywalls.
- No randomized loot boxes for gameplay stats.
- Transparent currencies and pricing.
- Free track must yield meaningful rewards.
- Seasonal content should return in future rotations if missed.

## 11. Risks & Mitigations

### Design Risks

- **Risk**: The idle loop feels too passive or too shallow.
  - **Mitigation**: Add interactive growth decisions, mutation choices, and expedition planning depth.

- **Risk**: Procedural dungeons become repetitive.
  - **Mitigation**: Build diverse room templates, biome-specific mechanics, and mutation-driven variation.

- **Risk**: Player onboarding is unclear due to hybrid systems.
  - **Mitigation**: Create a progressive tutorial that teaches planting, growth, adventurer dispatch, and harvesting in sequence.

### Technical Risks

- **Risk**: Procedural generation performance issues with large dungeon layouts.
  - **Mitigation**: Use efficient generation, chunked loading, and simplified render modes for idle preview.

- **Risk**: Save/load state consistency across many dynamic dungeon seeds.
  - **Mitigation**: Implement robust serialization and versioned save migration.

### Business Risks

- **Risk**: Monetization could be perceived as exploitative if convenience items feel required.
  - **Mitigation**: Ensure core progression is fully playable without purchase and keep convenience items optional.

- **Risk**: The RPG/idle hybrid niche may confuse players.
  - **Mitigation**: Emphasize the distinct value proposition: "Create the dungeon, then send heroes into it."

### Scope Risks

- **Risk**: Feature creep from too many dungeon mutation options or adventurer systems.
  - **Mitigation**: Start with a focused set of seeds, mutations, and hero classes for an MVP.

- **Risk**: Ambitious art direction extending beyond team capacity.
  - **Mitigation**: Use a stylized art pipeline with reusable modular assets and strong visual themes.
